Practical Effects

Recipes & Exercises for DIY Filmmaking

Base Recipes

Fake Blood

Objective: Create blood for spraying, smearing, or pooling effects.

Materials:
  • 12 cups light corn syrup (3 x 32 oz bottles)
  • 8 cups chocolate syrup
  • 6 cups strawberry syrup
  • 2 cups water
  • 1Â½ cups creamy peanut butter
  • Liquid red food coloring (5-second smooth pour)
  • Liquid blue food coloring (20 drops)
  • 3 cups non-toxic washable childrenâ€™s poster paint (classic red)
Steps:
  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the corn syrup, chocolate syrup, strawberry syrup, and water.
  2. Add the peanut butter and mix until smooth.
  3. Gradually stir in red food coloring, blue food coloring, and poster paint.
  4. Adjust the ratios to achieve the desired viscosity and color.
Filming Tips: Use a syringe or spray bottle for blood spurts. Capture in slow motion to emphasize texture and motion. Add lighting to highlight the shine.

Open Wounds

Objective: Create realistic open wounds for close-up shots.

Materials:
  • Glue (non-toxic school glue works well)
  • Toilet paper
  • Standard makeup items (foundation, red lipstick, black eyeshadow)
  • Fake blood (from above recipe)
Steps:
  1. Apply a thin layer of glue to the skin where the wound will go.
  2. Place a small piece of toilet paper on top and let it dry partially.
  3. Apply additional glue on top, shaping the toilet paper to create raised edges or tears.
  4. Blend foundation or skin-colored makeup over the dried glue and paper to match the skin tone.
  5. Add red lipstick or red makeup inside the wound to mimic fresh tissue.
  6. Dab black eyeshadow around the edges for depth.
  7. Finish with fake blood for realism.
Filming Tip: Use close-up shots to emphasize the wound detail.

Gore Realism Enhancements

Textures

  • Mix sand or fine gravel into fake blood for gritty, coagulated effects.
  • Use oatmeal soaked in blood for chunky or clotted appearances.

Layering

  • Apply thin layers of gelatin over fake blood to create "skin" peeling effects.
  • Add translucent silicone sheets to simulate deeper layers of flesh.

Temperature Effects

  • Warm fake blood for realistic fluidity.
  • Chill gelatin for stiffer, aged wounds.

DIY Creating Smoke

Dry Ice Smoke (Classic Fog)

Materials:
  • Dry ice (available at grocery or specialty stores)
  • Warm water (not boiling, around 60Â°C/140Â°F)
  • Insulated gloves or tongs (to handle dry ice safely)
  • Plastic or metal container
Steps:
  1. Fill the container with warm water (approximately halfway full).
  2. Using gloves or tongs, carefully add a small piece of dry ice into the water.
  3. The dry ice will sublimate, producing dense, low-lying fog.
  4. To sustain the effect, add more warm water as it cools.

Incense or Smoke Pellets

Use for: Smaller, controlled smoke sources like steaming pipes or mystical effects.

Steps: Light the incense or pellet on a safe tray. Use a handheld fan to guide the smoke.

Paper and Sawdust (Old-School Fire Simulation)

Use for: Simulating burning debris or aftermath of a fire (Outdoor only).

Steps: Burn paper and sawdust in a metal tray. Extinguish quickly to produce thick, dark smoke.

Haze in a Can

Use for: Subtle atmospheric effects or confined indoor spaces. Portable and clean.

Practical Exercises

Exercise: Creating a Realistic Prosthetic Hand

Materials: Alginate molding powder, Water, Silicone rubber kit, Silicone paint.
Steps:
  1. Mix alginate and water. Submerge hand to create mold (Wait 10-15 mins).
  2. Remove hand carefully. Pour mixed silicone rubber into the mold.
  3. Let set (5-6 hours). Remove and paint with flesh tones, veins, and bruises.

Exercise: Realistic Guts

Materials: Pantyhose, Paper towels, Fake blood.
Steps:
  1. Cut pantyhose into tubes. Stuff with crumpled paper towels.
  2. Twist and tie ends to mimic intestines.
  3. Soak in fake blood until saturated.

Exercise: Simulated Blood Spray

Materials: Small water gun or bulb syringe, Fake blood, Plastic sheeting.
Steps:
  1. Load water gun with blood.
  2. Practice spraying at angles for pressure control.
  3. Film in slow motion against a safe background.

Exercise: Melting Flesh Effect

Materials: Gelatin, Hot water, Flesh food coloring, Heat gun.
Steps:
  1. Create thick gelatin paste with flesh coloring. Apply to object.
  2. Use heat gun to melt it gently on camera.

Exercise: Simulated Rain

Materials: Hose with spray nozzle, Backlight.
Steps: Set nozzle to mist/rain. Backlight heavily so droplets show up on camera. Shoot at high shutter speed or slow motion.

Exercise: Water Ripple Effects

Materials: Shallow glass container, water, pebbles, light.
Steps: Fill container. Drop pebbles or use dropper for controlled ripples. Backlight for contrast.

Exercise: Controlled Fireburst

Safety: Outdoors only. Keep water/sand nearby.

Materials: Steel wool, Wire hanger, Lighter.
Steps: Attach steel wool to wire. Light it. Spin wire to create sparks.
